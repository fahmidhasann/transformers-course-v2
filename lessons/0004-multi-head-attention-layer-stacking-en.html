    Multi-Head Attention and Layer Stacking

    How do multiple Attention Heads and stacked layers construct deep semantic reasoning?

    Why Do We Need Multiple Attention Heads?

    We know that Self-Attention helps a word connect with other words to find context. But language is tricky! A single word can have many different relationships at the same time.

    Look at this sentence: "The cute dog chased the cat because it was running fast."

    Think about the word dog. It's doing several things at once:

    • It's being described as "cute".
    • It's performing the action "chased".
    • It's involved in a "why" scenario (because of the running cat).

    If the AI only had one way to pay attention (called Single-Head Attention), it would get confused trying to mix all these different relationships together. It's like trying to listen to 5 different conversations with one ear!

    The solution is Multi-Head Attention. Instead of one ear, the AI gets multiple "Heads" that listen in parallel. Each Head becomes a specialist:

    If the AI only had one way to pay attention, it would get confused trying to mix all these different relationships together.

    The solution is Multi-Head Attention. Instead of one, the AI gets multiple "Heads" that listen in parallel. Each Head becomes a specialist:

    • Head 1 (The Action Tracker): Focuses on who is doing what (e.g., dog & chased).
    • Head 2 (The Pronoun Detective): Figures out who "it" or "they" refers to.
    • Head 3 (The Describer): Links describing words to things (e.g., cute & dog).
    • Head 4 (The Order Keeper): Looks at how words follow each other.

    Dimension Splitting: A Clever Trick

    You might think running 8 "Heads" at the same time would make the computer 8 times slower. But the Transformer uses a clever trick to avoid this!

    Imagine a word's meaning is stored as a list of 512 numbers. If we have 8 Attention Heads, the AI doesn't give all 512 numbers to every Head.

    Instead, it splits the numbers equally:

    512 total numbers ÷ 8 Heads = 64 numbers per Head

    Each Head only looks at a small slice (64 numbers) to do its specialized job. When they are all done, they glue their answers back together into a single 512-number list.

    This allows the AI to think about the word from 8 different angles at the exact same speed as thinking from just 1 angle!

    Layer Stacking: Building Deep Understanding

    An AI model isn't just one single block of Multi-Head Attention. It's actually a massive tower of blocks stacked on top of each other. This is called Layer Stacking.

    For example, LLaMA-3 has 32 layers, and GPT-4 has over 100! Why so many? Because understanding language takes steps. The bottom layers handle simple things, and the top layers handle deep thinking:

    Top Layers: Big Picture & Reasoning
    Deals with abstract ideas, logic, sentiment, and the final answer.
    ↓
    Middle Layers: Grammar & Connections
    Figures out who is doing what, and what "it" refers to.
    ↓
    Bottom Layers: Basics & Spelling
    Looks at basic grammar rules, spelling, and word order.
    Middle Layers: Grammar & Connections

    Job: Figuring out how words connect to form a proper sentence.
